Jose Protacio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda

At the age of three, Rizal received his alphabet instruction from his mother in 1864-1865. But when Rizal was four years old, his sister Conception, the family's eighth child, passed away at the age of three. It was the first time he could recall crying for real.

In 1865, Rizal's father engaged Leon Monroy to teach him the basics of Latin while his mother taught him how to read and write.

In 1868 , Rizal and his father traveled to Antipolo to carry out their mother's promise to bring the kid to the Antipolo Virgin Shrine.

After that, they traveled to Manila to see his sister Saturnina, who was enrolled in La Concordia College in Sta. Ana.

In 1869, “Sa Aking Mga Kabata,” Rizal's first poem, was composed in Tagalog and featured the topic “Love of One's Language” when he was eight years old.

Rizal's Educational Background

Doña Teodora, Rizal's mother, served as his first instructor. Doña Teodora exhibited patience, diligence, and empathy. She also pushed Rizal to create poetry after seeing he had a gift for it.

Ateneo

In 1872, Rizal enrolled at Ateneo. He was a member of the Filipino, Mestizo, and Spanish-speaking class. But he was ranked last in the class since he was seen as inferior.

On March 23, 1877, Rizal graduated with five medals and a sobresaliente, or good, diploma. He was awarded a Bachelor of Arts degree.

Medical Studies in University of Sto. Thomas 1877 - 1882

He continued his education at UST and finished a year in Philosophy and Letters after deciding to shift to a medical course. He made the decision for him to be a physician and be able to treat his mother's failing eyesight. Fr. Pablo Ramon, the Father Rector of Ateneo whom he consulted recommended medicine.

He studied Cosmology, Metaphysics, Theodicy, and History of Philosophy.

Pre-Med Course

Just four, including Rizal, of the 28 young men enrolled at Ampliacion were given the opportunity to concurrently enroll in the preparatory course and the first year of medical school.

Additionally, Rizal completed his four years of hands-on medical training at the Hospital of San Juan de Dios in Intramuros.

In his last year at the university, Rizal finished the medicine course with the second-best result out of a shattered class of seven students, receiving the worldwide grade of Notable (Very Good) in every subject. Rizal then made the decision to study in Spain.

Academic Journey To Spain 1882 - 1885

His parents, particularly his mother, who would not let him leave, were among those who were unaware of his departure for Spain, along with the friars and Spanish authorities. He went by Jose Mercado to avoid being discovered.

He was the one Filipino passenger when he boarded the Salvadora on May 3, 1882, with the intention of traveling to Singapore.

He enrolled in two studies at Universidad Central de Madrid on November 3, 1882: Philosophy and Letters and Medicine.

He awarded the degree of Licentiate in Medicine on June 21, 1884.

Rizal also received better ratings when he completed his courses in philosophy and letters. On June 19, 1885, the Universidad Central de Madrid granted him an exceptional Licentiate degree in philosophy and letters.

Opthalmology study in Europe

Jose Rizal traveled to Germany and Paris to pursue his ophthalmology specialty. He selected this specialization out of all the branches because he wished to restore his mother's fading vision.

In 1885, Rizal, who was 24 years old at the time, left his studies at the Universidad Central de Madrid to pursue further training in ophthalmology in Paris.

He worked at the University Eye Hospital under the direction of Dr. Otto Becker, a distinguished German ophthalmologist.
